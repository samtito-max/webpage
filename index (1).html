About Us

History and the Theoretical Foundation of Montessori Education.
The Montessori method, developed by Dr. Maria Montessori in the early 1900s, was based on scientific observation of children's natural learning processes. The approach focuses on hands-on learning led by the child, with teachers and specially designed materials within a prepared environment that promotes independence, respect, and love for learning.

How Montessori Aligns with Whole Child Development
Montessori education cultivates cognitive, social, emotional, and physical growth. It educates children on developing critical thinking, self-discipline, and a lifelong love for learning by respecting their individuality to make choices through an exploratory environment.

Why Choose Montessori for Your Child?
We chose the Montessori model because it caters to the unique needs of the early learner through individual learning plans, specialized materials, and a focus on self-directed activity. We want to provide an environment where the children will feel valued and in control of their learning.

Our Curriculum and Learning Spaces

Infant Room: 6 weeks -12 months
Curriculum and Activities:
Sensory experiences, motor skill development, language exposure through songs and stories.
Theories and Philosophies: Attachment and secure relationships are emphasized.
Materials and Resources: Soft Mats, Sensory Toys, Mirrors, Low Shelves.
Volunteer Opportunities: Reading to babies, assisting with sensory activities.
At-Home Activities: Gentle massage, singing, safe exploration.
What to Expect: Gentle interactions, caregivers using respectful communication, infants exploring safely.

1-2 Year-Old Room
Curriculum and Activities: Practical life activities, language development, fine motor skills.
Theories and Philosophies: Sensitive periods for language and order as suggested by Montessori.
Materials and Resources: Child-sized furniture, language cards, simple puzzles.
Activities: practical life activities, reading Volunteer Opportunities.
At-Home Activities: Naming of objects, simple sorting games, routine-based tasks.
What to Expect: Promotion of independence with caregiver guidance of self-initiated activities.

3-4 Year-Old Room
Curriculum and Activities: Maths and language materials, cultural studies, art, music.
Theories and Philosophies: Montessori's Prepared Environment and Auto-education.
Materials and Resources: Bead chains, sandpaper letters, materials for art activities.
Volunteer Opportunities: Leading small group activities, cultural presentations.
At Home Activities: Counting objects, tracing letters, telling stories
What to Expect: Engaging learning experiences. Children working independently, collaboratively.

Get Involved
Volunteer Opportunities

We welcome parents to volunteer in various capacities to enhance the learning experience and foster a strong sense of community:
Infant Room:
Assist with Sensory Play: Engage infants with various sensory materials to stimulate their senses and promote development.
Read Stories: Share picture books and simple stories to help develop early language skills and foster a love for reading.
1-2 Year-Old Room:
Help with Practical Life Activities: Support toddlers in activities such as pouring, sorting, and cleaning, which build independence and fine motor skills.
Join Circle Time: Participate in group activities, including singing songs, playing simple instruments, and interactive storytelling to enhance social and language skills.
3-4 Year-Old Room:
Lead Cultural Presentations: Share your cultural heritage, traditions, and experiences to broaden the children's understanding of the world.
Support Art and Music Projects: Assist with creative activities such as painting, drawing, crafting, and playing musical instruments to encourage self-expression and creativity.

Your involvement as a volunteer helps create a rich, supportive learning environment for our children and strengthens the bond between home and school. We look forward to your participation and the unique contributions you will bring to our community.

Programs

At Harmony Montessori Early Learning Center, we offer a range of programs tailored to meet the developmental needs of children at different stages. Each program is designed to foster holistic development through a child-centered, hands-on approach, promoting independence, curiosity, and a love for learning.

Infant Program (6 weeks - 12 months)
Our Infant Program provides a safe, nurturing environment where babies can explore and develop at their own pace.
Toddler Program (1-2 Years)
Our Toddler Program encourages exploration and independence, helping children build foundational skills through practical life activities and interactive play.
Preschool Program (3-4 Years)
Our Preschool Program prepares children for the next stage of their educational journey by focusing on cognitive, social, and emotional development through a structured yet flexible curriculum.

Extended Care Program
Our Extended Care Program provides additional support for families needing care beyond regular school hours, with activities designed to complement the Montessori curriculum.
